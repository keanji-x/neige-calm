Global EV Battery Supply Chain — Q2 2026 Outlook

R Research Agent Updated 2h ago 14 sources ~6 min read

01 Executive summary

Battery cell pricing reversed three consecutive quarters of decline, climbing as cathode-grade lithium tightened across the first half of 2026. Refining capacity remains the single largest bottleneck, with the top three processors still accounting for a majority share of qualified output.1 Downstream, automakers continue to diversify supplier panels, but qualification cycles mean the effect on landed cost will lag by two to three quarters.2

02 Market dynamics

Pricing turned a corner in April

After bottoming in March, the blended cell price index moved higher for three consecutive months. The move was concentrated in NMC chemistries, where cathode input costs are most exposed to refined lithium, while LFP held comparatively flat.3
